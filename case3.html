WiKnow

An easy to use wine pairing food app for beginners.

The Problem

Beginner wine drinkers need help selecting a wine to pair with food.

The Solution

WiKnow gives users an easy, accessible tool to find wine pairings and learn more about wines.

My Role

User Research, Heuristic Evaluation, User Persona, Testing, Wireframing, Low-High Fidelity Prototyping

Tools

Figma, Miro, Canva, Google Suite, Zoom

Problem Statement

Beginner wine drinkers needs an easy, accessible resource to help them select a wine to pair with food because they don't have a good knowledge of wine yet, but want to learn more.

We believe creating a wine pairing app will give wine novice's an easy, accessible tool to find wine pairings and expand their wine knowledge.

What type of wine would pair well with roasted chicken?

Research - What We Learned

From the user interviews, we learned most wine drinkers wanted an easy way to find wine to pair with food, and learn more about that wine. Overall, users lacked knowledge to properly pair wines with food and they felt intimidated by online tools that were too advanced or overwhelming.

I struggle choosing wines due to my minimal knowledge of them.
- Jeff, 56
I would like to get better at wine pairing, but don't know where to begin.
- Scott, 31

Competitor Analysis

After looking at comparible competitors, it was determined that most of the competitors were not user friendly for beginner wine consumers. Even though the competitors offered wine pairing suggestions, they lacked the educational aspect for the novice consumer. This reinforced the importance of creating a simple yet effective wine pairing app that is also educational.

User Persona

Meet Edward.

Edward likes wine, but is unsure what kind of wine to order at a restaurant or buy to compliment his home cooked meal. He feels intimidated by too many selections of wine, and sometime embarrassed by his lack of knowledge. He wants an easy solution to learn about wine pairing options.

Wish List to Priority Matrix

Feature Prioritization Matrix

After ideating on possible solutions, a priority matrix was created to narrow in on the best solutions for today, and possible solutions for tomorrow.

Best Idea Now

Offering top 3 wine recommendations by food category.

Best Future Idea

Suggesting the best places to buy the wine.

User Flow

Learning from the user interviews, it was important to create a user-friendly flow chart that is easy to navigate. To begin, the user will select a food from the food category, and then choose the type of food preparation. To not overwhelm the user with too many options, 3 suggested wines are listed to pair with their selected food. To make the app an educational tool for the users, they have the option to learn more about their selected wine.

User Testing and Feedback

Usability test included 5 participates, and the test was performed via Zoom.

Test Objectives

  • Can the user navigate through our wine pairing flow and find a wine pairing for their meal.
  • Can the navigate to the learn more page.
  • Can understand the value of the learn more page? Too much or little information?
  • Is the content laid out in a clear and organized way?

User Feedback

Did we solve the problem?

Mission Complete

After our user testing, we collaborated as a team and discussed what we learned. We were happy to discover that all the users thought the app was easy to navigate, and they liked the option to learn more about the recommended wines. We also learned that users wanted the option to save their favorite wines.

Final Iterations

We went back and updated our user flow chart to include the “My Favorites” feature. We added a “My Favorites” button to the home page and the option to save a wine from the suggested wine list.
