Nonprofit Website Redesign

Creating a better user experience to help fosters save animals.

The Problem

Current website looks or unfinished which may deter potential adoptions or foster providers.

The Solution

Redesign the website to be organized and user friendly to attract new volunteers, donors, and fosters.

My Role

User Research, Heuristic Evaluation, User Persona, Testing, Wireframing, Low-High Fidelity Prototyping

Tools

Figma, Miro, Canva, Google Suite, Zoom

The Back Story

Southwest Metro Animal Rescue does not have a physical location or building. They are dependent on their online presence to reach their users.

Current website looks and feels unfinished which may deter potential adoptions or foster providers.

“Is this nonprofit still in business?”

The Solution

We believe redesigning the Southwest Metro Animal Rescue website will create an accessible platform to find loving homes for animals within the community.

User Persona

Usability Testing

For our user research, we interviewed 5 users and asked them to complete the task of finding animal foster information and fill out the online form.

We learned that all the users thought the website was difficult to navigate, and were disappointed in the 3 page foster application form. All the users stated they would not spend that much time on a form.

Our top priorities from our findings included redesigning the home page to make the purpose clear to users, redesign the application form that is easy to use, and organize the content that is user friendly.

Desktop Wireframing

The homepage content is organized in order of importance. From left to right, you see the logo, then the search bar, to the Donate button.

The main nav bar is separated into its own section. The main content area has 3 call to action cards, and the footer is organized into 4 small sections.

The redesigned application form collects simple but important information since we didn’t want to overwhelm the user with a complicated form.

Usability Testing and Findings

One area that we spent a good amount of time on was the color palette. We were unsure of the best colors for our design, so we did A/B testing to give us a better direction. As a result, our testers liked the orange primary interaction color. 

We also learned that the users liked the layout of the footer with helpful and organized links. 

For our usability testing, all users were able to find Foster information and complete the online form.

Style Guide

We decided on Open Sans font for its clean and modern typeface. We also incorporated animal pictures to promote an emotional connection. Overall, we wanted a simple and clean design that was eye pleasing for the best user experience.

Feedback and Iterations

Some of our final design changes included redoing the logo due to its small font size.  We changed the logo with a simple cat and dog image and increased the font.

Other changes included adding breadcrumbs for easier user navigation, and a dark blue backdrop to enhance the main navigation bar.

Lastly, we added animated confetti on the “Congratulations” page for fun interaction.

High Fidelity Prototype

High fidelity prototypes were created for desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Final Thoughts

We were unable to connect with SWMAR. We understand their volunteers also work full time jobs, therefore they may not have the staff to respond to inquiries that don’t relate to adoption or foster care.

However, we would like to continue to reach out to SWMAR and hopefully connect with them to get their feedback on the redesigned website.

Our goal is to help drive more traffic to their website and increase animal adoptions and foster providers.
